## TEMPORAL LANGUAGE ANCHORING

### 1450s–1500s (DA VINCI ERA)

**Period Context**: Renaissance Florence/Milan. Gutenberg printing press (1440). Leonardo born 1452.

**✓ USE THESE TERMS & FRAMING**
- Dissection, anatomy, proportion, harmony, mechanics, perspective
- The human form, the body, the eye, light and shadow
- Machines, pulleys, levers, screws, mechanisms
- Nature, observation, wonder, inquiry
- Patronage, commissions, masters, apprentices
- The Sforza court, Milan, Florence, Rome, France (historical context)
- Natural philosophy (not "science")
- Artistic endeavor, craftsmanship, technique

**✗ AVOID THESE (ANACHRONISTIC)**
- Science, scientist, technology, engineer (roles didn't exist)
- Electricity, chemical, atom, theory (concepts too modern)
- Psychology, biology, physics (disciplines not formalized)
- Laboratory, experiment (Renaissance: "investigations," "dissections")
- Patent, patent office, intellectual property (didn't exist)
- Machine gun, steam engine, industrial (anachronistic innovations)

**LANGUAGE FLAVOR (14–15% of sentences should reflect era)**
"Leonardo's investigations into proportion through anatomical dissection revealed..."
"The harmony of the human form reflected in his designs for mechanical devices..."
"His patrons in the Sforza court commissioned both artistic and military innovations..."

**Sample sentences (historically accurate voice):**
- "Through careful dissection of the human form, Leonardo understood the principles underlying both art and mechanics."
- "The proportion he observed in nature—the spiral of shells, the branching of rivers—appeared also in his architectural designs."
- "As a natural philosopher in the court of Milan, Leonardo pursued knowledge across disciplines considered separate by scholars of his time."

### 1880s–1920s (TESLA ERA)

**Period Context**: Industrial revolution (electricity). Edison vs. Tesla current wars. Radio invention. World Wars. Roaring 20s.

**✓ USE THESE TERMS & FRAMING**
- Electricity, electrical current, wireless transmission, oscillation
- Invention, engineer, inventor, patent, patent office
- Frequencies, resonance, vibration, oscillatory circuit
- Polyphase system, induction motor, transformer
- Alternating current (AC), direct current (DC)
- Wireless, telegraphy, radio, electromagnetic waves
- Laboratory, experiments, demonstration
- Technology, innovation, modernity
- Science, physicist (the role exists now)
- Mankind, humanity, human progress
- The future, transformation, revolution (in energy/technology)

**✗ AVOID THESE (ANACHRONISTIC)**
- Quantum, relativity (concepts but not accessible to Tesla's public discourse)
- Computer, digital, transistor, semiconductor
- Nuclear, atomic energy (not part of Tesla's work)
- Silicon, integrated circuit
- Internet, wireless internet (too modern)
- App, algorithm, software (completely modern)

**LANGUAGE FLAVOR (10–15% of sentences should reflect era)**
"Tesla's investigations into electrical resonance and wireless transmission were revolutionary..."
"The polyphase alternating current system transformed industrial power distribution..."
"His patent for the oscillating circuit demonstrated unprecedented control over electrical frequency..."

**Sample sentences (historically accurate voice):**
- "Through precise control of electrical frequency, Tesla conceived of transmitting energy wirelessly across great distances."
- "The oscillating circuit he developed responded to resonant frequencies with exactness that seemed almost impossible to his contemporaries."
- "Tesla envisioned a world where wireless transmission of energy would benefit all mankind—eliminating the need for power lines."
- "His investigations into the properties of electrical resonance suggested possibilities for human communication and energy transfer yet unexplored."
